Clinical Medical Assistant

Clinical Medical Assistants (CMAs) are integral members of the health care delivery team, qualified to work in the administrative office, examining room, and physician office laboratory. Their duties include taking and recording vital signs and medical histories, rooming patients and preparing them for exams, administering injections, drawing blood and performing commonly ordered lab tests.

Our program provides in-depth training to prepare students to join one of the fastest growing health occupations. The Fast Response Medical Assistant Course offers five certifications, including a complete Phlebotomy Certification!

This 5-month course (usually only 3 hours per day) covers clinical procedures such as CPR and first aid, sterile techniques, injections and immunizations, phlebotomy and venipuncture, electrocardiograms (ECG), patient preparation and interactions, and assisting with minor procedures. Equipment training includes autoclaves, centrifuges, blood pressure cuffs and stethoscopes, blood collection and diagnostic equipment, 12-lead ECG machines, oxygen tanks, nebulizers, spirometers, ambulatory and rehabilitation equipment, and much more.

Our 330 hours of didactic instruction and 200 hours of field externship experience will give you confidence, polished skills and contacts to get your career started!
