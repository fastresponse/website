Clinical Medical Assistant

American Medical Technologists define the medical assistant as an integral member of the health care delivery team, qualified by education and experience to work in the administrative office, the examining room, and the physician office laboratory. The medical assistant is also a liaison between the doctor and the patient and is of vital importance to the success of the medical practice. Medical Assistants enjoy exciting and multifaceted careers as duties vary greatly and can include taking and recording vital signs and medical histories, rooming patients and preparing them for exams, administering injections, drawing blood and performing commonly ordered lab tests.

Other schools only include venipuncture with their Medical Assistant courses, which is not enough training to draw blood in a hospital environment or work independently in a lab. However, Fast Response includes a complete PHLEBOTOMY CERTIFICATION with our program!

Our 5 month course (usually only 3 hours per day!) will prepare you for clinical procedures such as sterile techniques, assisting with minor procedures, injections, phlebotomy and electrocardiograms (ECG/EKG). A full month (160 hours) of field experience gives you confidence, polished skills and contacts to get your career started right. Our externship satisfies the work experience requirements of some of the major hospitals in the area.

According to the US Department of Labor, Medical Assisting is one of the fastest growing and most in-demand professions in the country!
